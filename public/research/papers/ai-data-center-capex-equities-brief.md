# Public Equities With the Highest Leverage to AI-Driven Data Center Capex (12-36 Months)

**Date:** March 26, 2026  
**Prepared for:** Adekunle Balogun  
**Scope:** Public equities most exposed to AI-driven data center capital expenditure across semiconductors, networking, power equipment, cooling, REITs, and infrastructure contractors.

## Executive Summary

AI data center capex remains in an expansion phase rather than a digestion phase. The clearest evidence is from the buyers: Alphabet guided to **$175-185 billion** of 2026 capex, Amazon to about **$200 billion** of 2026 capex, Meta to **$115-135 billion** of 2026 capex, and Microsoft continues to show AI infrastructure scaling through sharply higher cloud capex and property-and-equipment growth. OpenAI, Oracle, and SoftBank also continue to enlarge Stargate, with OpenAI describing roughly **7 GW of planned capacity** and more than **$400 billion** of planned investment across sites over three years. The demand signal is therefore broad, current, and still accelerating.

The equities with the highest operating leverage to this capex are not identical to the largest beneficiaries in absolute dollars. **NVIDIA** remains the largest absolute beneficiary, but the highest *equity leverage* over the next 12-36 months is concentrated in companies where AI data center demand is a large and rising share of revenue, backlog, or orders. On that basis, the most compelling names are **Vertiv, Marvell, Arista, Eaton, Quanta Services, Comfort Systems USA, Digital Realty, and Equinix**, with **Broadcom** and **NVIDIA** still essential core exposures.

The best risk-adjusted setup differs by category. In semis, **NVIDIA** and **Broadcom** offer the cleanest platform exposure, while **Marvell** offers higher sensitivity to incremental AI cluster build-outs. In networking, **Arista** is the clearest public Ethernet beneficiary. In physical infrastructure, **Vertiv** is the highest-beta name to power-and-thermal intensity, while **Eaton** offers a broader and somewhat lower-risk electrical pick-and-shovel exposure. In construction and deployment, **Quanta Services** and **Comfort Systems USA** are among the most direct public beneficiaries of power interconnection, electrical balance-of-plant, and mechanical buildout.

Valuation is the main constraint. Several of the highest-beta physical infrastructure names already trade at demanding multiples after large reratings. That does not break the thesis, but it changes the underwriting standard: upside now depends less on “AI is real” and more on whether revenue converts into sustained margins, backlog turns into cash, and power availability allows projects to commence on schedule.

## Assumptions and Method

### How “leverage” is defined

For this brief, “leverage to AI-driven data center capex” means a combination of:

1. Revenue or backlog directly tied to AI data center construction, equipment, or operations.
2. Incremental operating margin from that demand.
3. Visibility duration, including backlog, capacity reservations, or multiyear platform roadmaps.
4. Sensitivity to hyperscaler and AI-lab spending decisions over the next 12-36 months.

### Important assumptions

- Many companies do **not** disclose exact AI data center revenue shares. Where they do not, I infer exposure from order commentary, segment disclosures, customer mix, and management language.
- “Valuation sensitivity” is expressed mainly as **directional equity sensitivity** to revenue, margin, bookings, leased MW, or FFO outcomes, not as a precise target price model.
- Market valuation data below uses March 2026 market references from finance aggregators; those figures are current snapshots, not company disclosures.

## Current Landscape

### Demand is still being set by hyperscalers and AI model builders

The demand engine remains concentrated but widening:

- **Alphabet** said 2026 capex should be **$175-185 billion**, with Q4 2025 capex of **$27.9 billion** and 2025 capex of **$91.4 billion**; about **60%** of technical infrastructure capex in Q4 went to servers and **40%** to data centers and networking equipment.
- **Amazon** said 2026 capex should be about **$200 billion**, and explicitly tied the increase in property and equipment purchases to AI.
- **Meta** guided to **$115-135 billion** of 2026 capex, driven by infrastructure and “Meta Superintelligence Labs.”
- **Microsoft** continues to report cloud gross-margin pressure from AI infrastructure scaling and showed property and equipment rising to **$261.1 billion** at December 31, 2025 from **$205.0 billion** six months earlier.
- **OpenAI/Oracle/SoftBank** expanded Stargate to nearly **7 GW** of planned capacity and over **$400 billion** of investment under development across multiple U.S. sites.

### The constraint is shifting from GPU supply to full-stack deployment

In 2023-2024, the tightest bottleneck was accelerator availability. In 2026-2028, the bottleneck increasingly becomes:

- Deliverable utility power
- Switches and optics at AI-cluster scale
- Cooling architecture for higher rack density
- Electrical distribution gear
- Skilled labor and EPC capacity
- Powered land and pre-leased capacity in prime metros

That shift broadens the investable set beyond semis.

## Core Concepts

### Scale-up vs. scale-out

- **Scale-up** refers to tightly coupled accelerator clusters inside a pod or rack domain, where GPU-GPU interconnect, memory bandwidth, and thermal density matter most.
- **Scale-out** refers to cluster-to-cluster networking, broader Ethernet fabrics, optical interconnect, and campus infrastructure.

NVIDIA and Broadcom dominate scale-up economics. Arista, Marvell, optics suppliers, and data center physical infrastructure names benefit more from scale-out and site deployment.

### Power density is the key second derivative

The next wave of AI data center capex is not just more megawatts; it is more **megawatts per rack**. That matters because higher density increases spend on:

- Busways, switchgear, UPS, and power management
- Liquid cooling and coolant distribution
- Pumps, chillers, heat rejection, and control systems
- Design complexity and contractor scope

This favors **Vertiv, Eaton, Trane, Comfort Systems, and Quanta**.

### REIT economics are increasingly power economics

For **Digital Realty** and **Equinix**, AI upside is no longer just cabinet demand. It is mainly:

- Access to power
- Pre-leased development
- Yield on new projects relative to cost of capital
- Ability to monetize interconnection and higher-value AI adjacency

## Key Players by Category

## 1. Semiconductors

### NVIDIA (NASDAQ: NVDA)

**Why it matters:** Still the dominant economic beneficiary of AI data center capex.  
**Latest evidence:** Q4 FY2026 data center revenue was **$62.3 billion**, up **75% YoY**; full-year data center revenue was **$193.7 billion**, up **68%**.

**Upside drivers**

- Blackwell to Rubin platform cadence keeps replacement and expansion cycles active.
- AI spend is still platform-centric: compute, networking, storage, and software remain bundled around NVIDIA.
- Enterprise and sovereign AI buildouts widen demand beyond the top cloud buyers.

**Valuation sensitivity**

- Current valuation looks less extreme than peak sentiment suggests; market references show roughly **23x forward earnings** in March 2026.
- The stock is highly sensitive to any change in hyperscaler capex confidence, but estimate revisions can still move faster than multiple compression if revenue growth remains above 40%.

**Key risks**

- Export controls and China restrictions.
- Customer insourcing/custom ASICs.
- A faster-than-expected shift from training-heavy capex to more efficient inference architectures.

**Bottom line:** Highest absolute beneficiary; not the highest incremental beta.

### Broadcom (NASDAQ: AVGO)

**Why it matters:** Best second core semiconductor exposure after NVIDIA because it spans custom AI accelerators and AI networking.

**Latest evidence:** Broadcom said Q3 FY2025 AI revenue was **$5.2 billion**, up **63% YoY**, and expected Q4 FY2025 AI revenue of **$6.2 billion**.

**Upside drivers**

- Custom AI ASIC programs with hyperscalers.
- Ethernet switching and co-packaged optics roadmap.
- Custom silicon becomes more attractive as the largest buyers optimize cost per token and power efficiency.

**Valuation sensitivity**

- Less pure-play than NVIDIA because software contributes heavily.
- That mix moderates downside if AI capex pauses, but also slightly caps pure AI upside torque.

**Key risks**

- Customer concentration.
- Program timing on custom XPU ramps.
- Competition from internal silicon teams and merchant alternatives.

**Bottom line:** Best large-cap hedge against a partial shift away from all-NVIDIA architectures.

### Marvell (NASDAQ: MRVL)

**Why it matters:** One of the highest-beta semiconductor names to incremental AI data center capex.

**Latest evidence:** FY2026 revenue reached **$8.195 billion**, up **42% YoY**; management attributed the growth to robust AI demand and said design wins hit an all-time record.

**Upside drivers**

- Exposure to AI interconnect, custom silicon attach, electro-optics, and storage connectivity.
- Record bookings and design wins suggest the revenue wave extends beyond the current year.
- If Ethernet AI clusters keep taking share, Marvell’s attach rate can compound.

**Valuation sensitivity**

- Market references show roughly **24x forward earnings** and around **10x sales**.
- Because the base business is smaller, incremental AI design-win conversion can drive outsized EPS revisions.

**Key risks**

- Execution risk on program ramps.
- Customer concentration and lumpier revenue recognition.
- Competitive pressure from Broadcom, NVIDIA, and internal hyperscaler designs.

**Bottom line:** One of the cleanest high-beta semi expressions of continued AI cluster buildout.

## 2. Networking

### Arista Networks (NYSE: ANET)

**Why it matters:** Best public Ethernet networking beneficiary of AI scale-out.

**Latest evidence:** 2025 revenue reached **$9.0 billion**; Q4 2025 revenue was **$2.488 billion**, up **28.9% YoY**. Management said 2025 exceeded AI networking goals.

**Upside drivers**

- AI back-end Ethernet adoption.
- 800G and next-gen routing platforms.
- Large cloud buyers standardizing on Ethernet-based AI fabrics.

**Valuation sensitivity**

- Market references show about **43x forward earnings** and roughly **19-20x EV/revenue**.
- That premium leaves the stock very sensitive to any sign that AI networking growth is being pulled forward.

**Key risks**

- Slower Ethernet share gains vs. NVIDIA InfiniBand or alternative architectures.
- Concentration in a few large cloud customers.
- Margin pressure if product mix shifts toward newer systems.

**Bottom line:** Best pure public networking exposure, but with a premium multiple that requires execution.

## 3. Power Equipment and Thermal Management

### Vertiv (NYSE: VRT)

**Why it matters:** Probably the single highest public-equity beta to AI data center physical infrastructure.

**Latest evidence:** Q4 2025 organic orders grew **252%**; book-to-bill was about **2.9x**; backlog rose to **$15.0 billion**, up **109% YoY**; 2026 sales guidance implies **27-29%** organic growth.

**Upside drivers**

- Liquid cooling, power train, integrated thermal systems, and service.
- Higher rack density structurally increases Vertiv content per MW.
- Backlog and order growth give unusual visibility for an industrial name.

**Valuation sensitivity**

- March 2026 market references imply about **45-49x forward earnings** and about **10x sales**.
- This is the clearest case where the business is excellent but expectations are already very high.
- If orders normalize from extraordinary levels, the stock can derate even if fundamentals remain strong.

**Key risks**

- Expectations risk after a massive rerating.
- Project timing slippage due to utility delays or customer redesigns.
- Competitive intensity as large HVAC/electrical incumbents push deeper into liquid cooling.

**Bottom line:** Highest-beta physical infrastructure name; most attractive on pullbacks rather than momentum alone.

### Eaton (NYSE: ETN)

**Why it matters:** Best diversified electrical equipment exposure to AI data center capex.

**Latest evidence:** Eaton said Electrical Americas rolling 12-month orders were up **16%** in Q4 2025, driven by data center momentum; Electrical sector backlog was up **29% YoY**.

**Upside drivers**

- Switchgear, breakers, busway, PDUs, and power quality systems.
- Broad exposure to utility interconnection and on-site electrical buildout.
- Less single-product risk than cooling specialists.

**Valuation sensitivity**

- Premium industrial multiple, but lower expectation risk than Vertiv.
- The key swing factors are Electrical Americas order growth and margin durability rather than a pure AI multiple.

**Key risks**

- Capacity constraints or component bottlenecks.
- Tariff/input cost effects.
- If AI capex slows, ETN still has many other end markets, which softens downside but also reduces pure upside torque.

**Bottom line:** Best lower-risk power pick-and-shovel name.

### Trane Technologies (NYSE: TT)

**Why it matters:** Increasingly relevant to liquid cooling and turnkey thermal systems.

**Latest evidence:** Trane launched a thermal management reference design for NVIDIA AI factories and, in 2026, closed acquisitions of **Stellar Energy** and **LiquidStack** to build end-to-end data center cooling capability.

**Upside drivers**

- Direct-to-chip and liquid cooling penetration.
- Modular cooling plants and turnkey deployments.
- Growing strategic tie-ins with NVIDIA reference architectures.

**Valuation sensitivity**

- Lower direct AI leverage than Vertiv, but potentially underappreciated as thermal architectures shift.

**Key risks**

- Integration execution on acquisitions.
- Data center cooling remains a minority piece of a broader HVAC portfolio.

**Bottom line:** More of an “emerging leverage” name than a pure-play.

## 4. REITs and Digital Infrastructure

### Digital Realty (NYSE: DLR)

**Why it matters:** One of the cleanest listed ways to own powered land, wholesale data center capacity, and AI-related leasing.

**Latest evidence:** Digital Realty reported **record bookings and backlog** in its Q4 2025 materials and guided 2026 core FFO/share to **$7.90-8.00**, roughly **8%** growth at the midpoint.

**Upside drivers**

- Hyperscale leasing and AI-driven power demand in constrained metros.
- Backlog conversion into revenue and FFO.
- Strong pricing if capacity remains effectively sold out in top markets.

**Valuation sensitivity**

- REIT upside is especially sensitive to leased MW conversion, development yield spreads, and cost of capital.
- If rates fall while AI demand remains strong, DLR can enjoy both operating and multiple tailwinds.

**Key risks**

- Power delays delay lease commencements and cash flow.
- Higher rates or equity issuance pressure development returns.
- Customer concentration is higher than at Equinix.

**Bottom line:** Better pure hyperscale/wholesale AI exposure than Equinix, with more balance-sheet and concentration risk.

### Equinix (NASDAQ: EQIX)

**Why it matters:** Highest-quality listed data center platform, with AI exposure plus interconnection and enterprise adjacency.

**Latest evidence:** Equinix reported record bookings in Q3 2025 and said land acquisitions support **900+ MW** of retail and xScale capacity at full build-out.

**Upside drivers**

- xScale expansion for larger AI and cloud deployments.
- Interconnection and enterprise AI adjacency can increase value per deployed workload.
- Strong balance-sheet access and globally scarce metro footprint.

**Valuation sensitivity**

- Market references imply a high multiple relative to REIT peers.
- Because EQIX is a quality compounder, it usually rerates less dramatically than DLR on cyclical AI enthusiasm.

**Key risks**

- AI upside is somewhat diluted by the broader retail colocation mix.
- New capacity takes time to monetize.

**Bottom line:** Highest-quality REIT, but less pure AI torque than DLR.

## 5. Infrastructure Contractors

### Quanta Services (NYSE: PWR)

**Why it matters:** Direct leverage to the grid, transmission, substations, and power-enablement layer that AI campuses need.

**Latest evidence:** Q3 2025 RPO reached **$21.0 billion** and total backlog **$39.2 billion**; management cited electric segment acceleration and selection by NiSource for power-generation and grid solutions serving a large-load customer.

**Upside drivers**

- Utility upgrades and interconnection work become mandatory as AI campuses scale.
- Gas generation, substations, and transmission spend rise with large-load demand.
- Exposure extends beyond data centers into the broader grid response to AI load growth.

**Valuation sensitivity**

- Premium contractor multiple already reflects strong power-infrastructure demand.
- Upside depends on data center-linked utility awards staying incremental rather than replacing other backlog.

**Key risks**

- Execution and labor availability.
- Regulatory or permitting delays on transmission and generation.

**Bottom line:** Best public way to own the “outside-the-fence” power buildout.

### Comfort Systems USA (NYSE: FIX)

**Why it matters:** Highly levered to mechanical, electrical, and HVAC deployment in mission-critical facilities.

**Latest evidence:** Backlog climbed from **$5.99 billion** at year-end 2024 to **$6.89 billion** in Q1 2025 and **$8.12 billion** in Q2 2025; management repeatedly highlighted extraordinary demand in core markets through 2026.

**Upside drivers**

- Mechanical and electrical complexity rises with liquid cooling and denser data halls.
- Fast cycle-time on project awards can translate into faster revenue conversion than some OEM categories.

**Valuation sensitivity**

- One of the market’s favorite AI-infrastructure contractors; multiple is no longer cheap.
- Because margins are already strong, even a modest slowdown in backlog growth could compress the stock.

**Key risks**

- Project concentration.
- Labor tightness and execution risk.
- Contractor stocks can over-earn temporarily at cycle peaks.

**Bottom line:** Very high torque, but also one of the easiest places for the market to overprice perfection.

## Recent Developments That Matter

- **Hyperscaler guidance has kept rising**, not flattening.
- **Stargate has moved from concept to multi-site development**, reinforcing demand visibility for power, cooling, and contractors.
- **Vertiv order acceleration** suggests the physical layer of AI deployment is now in a sharp upcycle.
- **Trane’s acquisitions of LiquidStack and Stellar Energy** show incumbent HVAC players are moving aggressively into liquid cooling.
- **GE Vernova’s gas power backlog and slot reservations rose from 62 GW to 83 GW** by Q4 2025, supporting the view that data center power demand is spilling into generation equipment and grid investment.
- **Data center REITs are increasingly differentiating on powered land access**, not just space.

## Opportunities

### Most practical ways to express the theme

- Use **NVIDIA + Broadcom** as the core semiconductor barbell.
- Add **Arista** for Ethernet networking exposure.
- Add **Vertiv** or **Eaton** depending on desired risk level.
- Add **Quanta** or **Comfort Systems** for deployment and labor-capacity scarcity.
- Add **Digital Realty** if you want AI demand plus an interest-rate-sensitive real asset.

### Where the market may still be underestimating upside

- Power-enablement spend outside the data hall.
- Cooling content per MW as rack density rises.
- The persistence of AI inference capex after training clusters are built.
- Utility and generation spend needed to make announced campuses actually operable.

## Risks

### Macro and theme risks

- Hyperscalers could optimize more aggressively and slow capex growth after 2026.
- Model efficiency gains could reduce required compute and MW per unit of output.
- Power and permitting bottlenecks could delay revenue conversion even if demand remains intact.

### Equity-specific risks

- **Semis:** export controls, customer insourcing, rapid product transitions.
- **Networking:** buyer concentration and architecture shifts.
- **Power/cooling:** expectation risk after large reratings.
- **REITs:** rates, financing costs, and delayed commencements.
- **Contractors:** labor, claims, project concentration, and margin mean reversion.

## Open Questions

1. How much of 2026-2027 spend is true net-new demand versus pull-forward?
2. Will Ethernet gain enough share in AI fabrics to expand Arista/Marvell upside materially beyond current expectations?
3. How quickly will liquid cooling become standard rather than premium?
4. Which companies best monetize the power bottleneck: electrical OEMs, utilities, REITs, or contractors?
5. Do custom AI ASICs broaden the supply chain opportunity, or mainly redistribute economics away from NVIDIA?

## What to Monitor in the Next 12 Months

- Hyperscaler capex guidance changes from **Microsoft, Meta, Amazon, and Alphabet**.
- Order growth and book-to-bill at **Vertiv** and **Eaton**.
- AI revenue progression at **Broadcom** and design-win conversion at **Marvell**.
- AI networking commentary and cloud customer concentration at **Arista**.
- Lease commencements, backlog conversion, and powered land commentary at **Digital Realty** and **Equinix**.
- Backlog quality and large-load utility project awards at **Quanta** and **Comfort Systems**.
- Utility power availability, gas turbine reservations, and large-load interconnection queues.
- Evidence of capex broadening from top-4 hyperscalers into enterprise, sovereign, and neo-cloud buyers.

## Actionable Next Steps

1. Build a tiered watchlist with three buckets: `core compounders` (NVDA, AVGO, ETN), `high-beta infrastructure` (VRT, MRVL, ANET, PWR, FIX), and `real-asset exposure` (DLR, EQIX).
2. Underwrite each name against one primary KPI rather than a generic AI narrative: `data center revenue` for NVDA, `AI semiconductor revenue` for AVGO, `orders/book-to-bill` for VRT, `Electrical Americas orders` for ETN, `leased MW/backlog` for DLR, and `backlog/RPO` for PWR and FIX.
3. Stress-test valuation using two scenarios: `capex keeps accelerating through 2027` and `capex growth slows but remains positive`; the latter is the more important scenario because many names are no longer cheap.
4. Prefer pullback entry points in the most crowded physical-infrastructure names, especially VRT and FIX, where expectations already discount a large portion of the bull case.
5. Track power-bottleneck beneficiaries separately from compute beneficiaries; if AI spend shifts from GPUs toward site-enablement, ETN, PWR, DLR, and GEV can outperform even if semiconductor sentiment cools.
6. Reassess the thesis after each earnings cycle using actual conversion metrics, not press releases: backlog burn, lease commencements, gross margin, and cash conversion matter more than AI-themed announcements.

## Key Takeaways

1. The AI data center capex cycle is still expanding, with hyperscaler and AI-lab guidance supporting a multi-year buildout.
2. The highest *absolute* beneficiary is NVIDIA, but the highest *equity beta* is in physical infrastructure names such as Vertiv, Comfort Systems, and Quanta.
3. Eaton is the cleanest lower-risk power equipment exposure; Vertiv is the highest-risk/highest-reward power-and-cooling expression.
4. Marvell and Arista are among the best leveraged ways to play Ethernet-based AI cluster growth.
5. Digital Realty offers more direct hyperscale AI torque than Equinix; Equinix offers higher quality and lower concentration risk.
6. The next bottleneck is increasingly power, cooling, and deployment capacity, not just GPUs.
7. Valuation discipline now matters as much as theme selection.

## Final View

If I had to isolate the names with the highest 12-36 month leverage to AI data center capex, I would separate them into:

- **Best core exposures:** NVDA, AVGO, ETN
- **Best high-beta exposures:** VRT, MRVL, ANET, PWR, FIX
- **Best REIT exposures:** DLR first for torque, EQIX first for quality

The most important non-consensus point is that the next leg of AI equity outperformance may come less from “more GPUs” alone and more from the harder-to-replicate layers around **power, cooling, and grid-enablement**.
